What Is Manus AI? (And Why You Should Care)
Lesson 1.1

From Google to AI Agents: A New Way of Working

🎯 Lesson Objectives
  • Understand the difference between a search engine and an AI agent
  • Explain in your own words what "autonomous" means in the context of Manus
  • List 3 things Manus can do that Google cannot

For years, the internet worked like a library. You walked in, asked the librarian (Google) where to find something, and then you did all the work yourself — reading, comparing, copying, deciding. AI agents like Manus change this completely.

Think of Manus not as a search engine, but as a capable assistant that can think, plan, take action, and report back. You describe the outcome you want. Manus figures out the steps and executes them — browsing the web, reading documents, writing content, organising information — all without you lifting a finger.

AI AgentA system that can take a goal and break it into tasks, using tools like web browsing, file creation, and code execution to complete them.
AutonomousWorking independently with minimal human input. You set the goal; it handles the how.
PromptYour instruction to Manus. The better your prompt, the better your result.
WorkflowA sequence of tasks Manus performs in order to complete a larger goal.
Manus is not magic — it is a very organised, very fast assistant. The better you describe what you want, the more it feels like magic.
Lesson 1.2

What Manus Can (and Cannot) Do

🎯 Lesson Objectives
  • Identify at least 5 tasks Manus can automate for you
  • Recognise the current limitations of Manus so you set realistic expectations

What Manus CAN do:

  • Research & Summarise: Browse websites, read articles, and give you concise summaries on any topic
  • Write & Edit: Draft emails, reports, essays, plans, and social media posts
  • Organise Information: Sort data, create tables, generate schedules and to-do lists
  • Execute Multi-Step Tasks: Complete long sequences of actions without needing your input at each step
  • Analyse Documents: Read and interpret PDFs, spreadsheets, and text documents
  • Generate Creative Content: Write stories, scripts, captions, marketing copy, and more
  • Code Simple Scripts: Write basic scripts to automate repetitive computer tasks

Current Limitations:

  • Cannot access your private accounts unless you connect them
  • Cannot perform physical actions in the real world
  • May make errors — always verify important outputs
  • Does not have real-time internet access by default in all modes
  • Cannot guarantee 100% accuracy on sensitive data (finances, medical)
Lesson 1.3

How Manus Reads Your Instructions

Manus processes natural language — meaning you write to it the way you would write to a smart colleague. It does not require codes, commands, or programming. But there is a skill to it. Vague instructions produce vague results.

❌ Vague Instruction✅ Clear Instruction
"Summarise the news""Summarise the top 5 tech and business news stories from today, in 2 sentences each, formatted as a bullet list"
"Help me with my emails""Read my last 10 unread emails and categorise them as: Urgent, Needs Reply, or Read Later. Then draft a one-line reply for the urgent ones."

Prompt Engineering for Beginners

Prompting is the most important skill in this entire course. Everything else — the tools, the automations, the workflows — lives or dies based on the quality of your instructions.

Lesson 3.1

What Is a Prompt?

A prompt is simply what you type to Manus. But not all prompts are equal. A great prompt is specific, contextual, and outcome-focused. If you hired a personal assistant on their first day, you would not say "do something useful." You would explain the task, give context, and describe what success looks like. Same principle here.

Lesson 3.2

The 5-Part Prompt Formula

🎯 Lesson Objectives
  • Memorise the 5-Part Prompt Formula
  • Write at least 3 prompts using the formula for tasks in your own life
PartWhat It MeansExample Phrase
1. ROLETell Manus who or what to act as"Act as a productivity coach…"
2. CONTEXTGive background information about your situation"…I'm a university student with 3 exams this week…"
3. TASKState clearly what you want Manus to do"…create a 5-day study schedule for me…"
4. FORMATSpecify how you want the output structured"…formatted as a table with time blocks…"
5. CONSTRAINTAdd any limits or requirements"…I can only study between 6pm and 10pm daily."
📋 COMPLETE FORMULA PROMPT
Act as a productivity coach. I am a busy professional working from home with two kids. Create a realistic daily schedule for me that balances deep work, meetings, family time, and exercise. Format it as an hour-by-hour timetable from 6am to 10pm. I must do school drop-off at 8am and pick-up at 3pm.
Lesson 3.3

Common Prompting Mistakes (and Fixes)

❌ Mistake✅ Fix
Too vague: "Write something about marketing""Write a 200-word Instagram caption promoting a palm oil product for Nigerian retail buyers. Tone: trustworthy and direct."
No format: "Give me a list of ideas""Give me 10 content ideas for LinkedIn. Present as a numbered list with a one-line description for each."
Too many things: "Write my email, schedule my week, and plan my meals"Split into 3 separate prompts — one per task. Manus works best with focused instructions.
No context: "Reply to this email""Reply to this email from a client who asked about delivery timelines. Our delivery takes 3-5 business days. Keep the tone professional but friendly."
First draft accepted: Taking the output without questionAlways follow up: "Make this shorter", "Use a more formal tone", "Add more specific examples."

Lesson 4.2

Email Triage, Inbox Merging & Priority Management

🎯 Lesson Objectives
  • Merge all your Gmail accounts into one unified inbox using Forwarding and POP/IMAP
  • Use Manus to sort, prioritise, and draft replies to emails
  • Set up a reusable email triage routine across all your accounts

Most people managing multiple Gmail accounts waste enormous time switching between inboxes. Before Manus can triage your email properly, you need a single unified inbox it can see. Gmail's built-in Forwarding and POP/IMAP settings make this free and permanent.

Part A — Merging Your Gmail Inboxes (Do This First)

Set one Gmail account as your Primary Inbox — the one Manus connects to and where all your emails land. Every other account becomes a Sending Account that forwards its mail to the Primary.

Method 1: Automatic Forwarding (Recommended)

Step 1 — Open Your Secondary Gmail AccountLog into the Gmail you want to forward FROM (e.g. your personal Gmail). This is NOT your primary account.
Step 2 — Open Gmail SettingsClick the gear icon ⚙ at the top right → select "See all settings".
Step 3 — Go to "Forwarding and POP/IMAP" TabYou will see this tab along the top of the Settings page. Click it.
Step 4 — Add a Forwarding AddressUnder "Forwarding", click "Add a forwarding address". Type in your PRIMARY Gmail address. Click Next → Proceed → OK.
Step 5 — Confirm the Forwarding RequestGmail sends a confirmation email to your Primary inbox. Open it and click the confirmation link.
Step 6 — Activate ForwardingBack in the secondary account's Forwarding settings, select "Forward a copy of incoming mail to [your primary address]". In the dropdown next to it, choose "Keep Gmail's copy in the Inbox". Click Save Changes.
Step 7 — Repeat for Every Secondary AccountIf you have 3 Gmail accounts, do Steps 1-6 twice. All roads now lead to your Primary inbox.
You can also set up Gmail to SEND AS other addresses from your Primary inbox. Go to Settings → Accounts and Import → "Add another email address". This means you can reply from work@gmail.com even while inside your personal inbox.

Method 2: POP/IMAP (For Pulling in Older Emails)

POP lets you pull all existing emails — including older ones — from a secondary account into your Primary. IMAP syncs in real time. When connecting Manus to Gmail, enable IMAP in Settings → "Forwarding and POP/IMAP" → IMAP Access → Enable IMAP.

Step 1 — Enable POP in the Secondary AccountIn the secondary Gmail → Settings → "Forwarding and POP/IMAP" tab → Under "POP Download", select "Enable POP for all mail". Click Save Changes.
Step 2 — Add the Secondary Account to Your PrimaryIn your Primary Gmail → Settings → "Accounts and Import" tab → Under "Check mail from other accounts", click "Add a mail account".
Step 3 — Configure the POP ConnectionUse: Server: pop.gmail.com | Port: 995 | Use SSL: Yes. Enter the password for the secondary account. Click Add Account.
Step 4 — Handle Two-Factor AuthenticationIf the secondary account uses 2FA, create an App Password: Google Account settings → Security → App Passwords → Generate one for "Mail" and use it as the password.
Step 5 — Label Incoming EmailsGmail will ask if you want to label incoming mail. Say Yes and create a label like "From: Work" or "From: Business". This keeps your unified inbox organised when Manus reads it.

Part B — Using Manus to Triage Your Unified Inbox

📧 PASTE & TRIAGE
[Paste your email list or connect your inbox] Read these emails and categorise each one as: URGENT (needs reply today), ACTION NEEDED (needs a task or reply within 3 days), or FYI (read only). Present as a table with: Sender | Subject | Category | Suggested Action.
📧 MULTI-ACCOUNT TRIAGE
I have just merged multiple Gmail inboxes. Some emails are labelled "From: Work", "From: Business", and some are personal. Triage my inbox treating these as separate contexts: - Work emails: prioritise by deadline and sender seniority - Business emails: prioritise by revenue impact - Personal: flag only truly urgent items Present as three separate tables, one per account context.
📧 DRAFT QUICK REPLIES
I have received the following emails. [Paste emails] For each one that needs a reply, draft a concise professional response. My tone is warm but direct. Keep replies under 80 words unless the situation genuinely requires more detail.
📧 INBOX ZERO PLAN
I have [X] unread emails across my merged inbox. Help me create a 30-minute inbox zero session plan. Tell me the order in which to tackle them, what qualifies as "done", and give me templates for the 3 most common replies I'll likely need to send.
Save your triage prompt as a Manus template. Each morning, open Manus, run the triage prompt once, and you have a complete picture of all your accounts in under 2 minutes — no inbox switching required.

Building Multi-Step Workflows

Workflows are where the real power is. A workflow chains multiple tasks together so that one instruction triggers a whole sequence of actions. This is where you stop saving minutes and start saving hours.

Lesson 7.1

What Is a Workflow?

Manual Workflow (45 min)Manus Workflow (5 min)
1. Check email
2. Identify urgent messages
3. Draft replies
4. Update task list
5. Adjust schedule		 "Review my emails, flag urgent ones, draft replies, add any new tasks to my list, and tell me if my schedule needs adjusting."
Lesson 7.2

Chaining Tasks in Manus

⚙️ CONTENT CREATION WORKFLOW
I need to create a LinkedIn post about [TOPIC]. Complete this full workflow: Step 1: Research the topic and give me 5 key insights Step 2: Draft 3 different LinkedIn post options (under 250 words each) using different hooks Step 3: Rate each option out of 10 and explain why Step 4: Combine the best elements of all three into one final post Step 5: Suggest the best day and time to post this for maximum engagement
⚙️ PROJECT KICKOFF WORKFLOW
I am starting a new project: [PROJECT DESCRIPTION] Run a full project kickoff for me: Step 1: Identify potential challenges and risks Step 2: Break the project into phases with estimated timelines Step 3: List all the resources, tools, or people I will need Step 4: Write a project brief (1 page) Step 5: Create a 30-day action plan with weekly milestones
⚙️ LEARNING WORKFLOW
I want to learn [SKILL/TOPIC] in [TIMEFRAME]. Create a full learning plan: Step 1: Break down the skill into component parts (beginner to advanced) Step 2: Find and list the best free resources for each part Step 3: Build a weekly study schedule that fits [MY AVAILABILITY] Step 4: Design 3 practical exercises I can do to apply the learning Step 5: Create a self-assessment test I can take at the end of Week 2
